How the System Works

An automated pipeline that goes from raw RSS feeds to a published, interactive newsletter website — fully hands-off except for a weekly quality check.

What the system does automatically

Every week, a Python script runs on the Beijing side and does the following in sequence — no human intervention needed until the final review step.

📡
Fetches news from 30+ sources

Pulls articles from RSS feeds across financial media, government releases, central bank announcements, and fintech outlets — covering the past 7 days.

🤖
AI filters & summarizes

Claude AI scores each article for relevance to Mexico's economy and fintech landscape, removes duplicates, rewrites headlines for clarity, and generates concise summaries.

🌐
Publishes to the website

The generated newsletter is pushed to GitHub and the live website updates within ~1 minute. No manual upload needed.

📊
Tracks source health

An Excel report (Source Report) is generated alongside the newsletter, showing which RSS sources succeeded or failed, and how many articles were fetched from each.

The newsletter website

The newsletter is published at the following URL. It is publicly accessible — no login needed to read it.

https://hansonkjzheng-source.github.io/mexico-newsletter/

The website is organized into three topic sections:

🏛️
Macro & Policy

Central bank decisions, government fiscal policy, regulatory announcements, GDP data, inflation, and broader economic signals.

🏦
Financial Players

Bank earnings, fintech funding rounds, payments companies, neobanks, remittance players, and key industry partnerships.

🌎
International Impact

US-Mexico trade dynamics, tariff news, cross-border investment flows, and global macro events that affect Mexico.

Reading & navigating the newsletter

The newsletter is designed to be quick to scan. Each story is collapsed by default — click to expand and read the full summary and sources.

🔽
Expandable cards

Click any news card to expand and read the full summary. Click again to collapse. Each card shows the headline by default.

⬇ ⬆
Expand / Collapse All

The Expand All button at the top opens all stories at once for a full read. Click again to collapse all.

🔗
Source links

Each card shows one or more source links — click to open the original article for verification or deeper reading.

🧭
Section navigation

The three category pills at the top of the page scroll directly to each section — useful on longer newsletters.

Weekly Collaboration SOP

How the Beijing and Mexico teams work together every week to produce, review, and distribute the newsletter.

Weekly rhythm — Beijing & Mexico

The two teams operate across a 13-hour time difference. Beijing runs the script Wednesday afternoon, which is early Wednesday morning in Mexico City — so by the time the Mexico team starts work, the newsletter is already live and ready for review.

🇨🇳 Beijing (CST)
🇲🇽 Mexico City (CST)
Wednesday · 2–5 PM (BJT)
🤖 Run Python script

Fetch news → AI filter → generate newsletter → auto-push to website. Entire process takes ~10 minutes.

Wednesday · 1–4 AM (MXT)
🌐 Website goes live

Newsletter auto-published at hansonkjzheng-source.github.io/mexico-newsletter/

Wednesday morning (MXT)
✅ Mexico team reviews & publishes

Review content, make edits, then publish the final version before distributing to stakeholders.

Wednesday (after review)
📤 Distribute to group

Download Word doc → copy title summary (page 1) → send to the sharing group.

Mexico team — Wednesday review checklist

After Beijing pushes the newsletter, the Mexico team follows these steps. Unlock the editor first by clicking the 🔒 lock icon (bottom right) and entering the password.

1
Check Source Report for failed RSS sources

In edit mode, click ⬇ Source Report to download the Excel file. Open it and look for sources marked as FAIL — these feeds could not be fetched automatically this week.

For any failed sources that are critical to your coverage, manually browse those outlets and note any important stories you want to add. You can add them in Step 3.

2
Review AI-generated stories for accuracy

Read through the newsletter on the website. For any story that seems surprising, unclear, or potentially incorrect:

→ Click the source link(s) on that card to open the original article(s) and cross-verify the content.
→ If the headline or summary is inaccurate, hover over the card → click the edit icon (✏️) → correct the title or body text.

3
Remove, add, or re-categorize stories

To delete an inappropriate or irrelevant story: hover the card → click the delete icon (🗑️).

To add a story you collected manually (e.g. a product update notification from an app, an industry contact tip, or a story from a failed source): use the edit icon on an existing card to repurpose it, or ask Beijing to add a new card template if needed.

To move a story to a different section or change its order: drag the handle (⠿) on the left side of the card to reposition it within its section. Note that moving between sections (e.g. from Macro to Financial Players) currently requires deleting and re-adding.

4
Publish the reviewed newsletter

Once you are satisfied with the content, click the ⬆ Publish button in the bottom toolbar. The website will update within a few seconds — all edits go live for anyone who visits the URL.

You can verify by opening the site in a new incognito tab.

5
Download Word doc and send the summary to the group

In edit mode, click ⬇ Word to download the Word document. Open it in Word.

Page 1 contains all story titles organized by section, numbered, with links to the full website at the bottom — this is the summary you paste into the sharing group.
Page 2 contains full titles + summaries for reference if colleagues want more detail.

Copy the content from Page 1 and send it to the distribution group.

Quick reference checklist

Use this as a mental checklist each Wednesday:

  • → Unlock editor — click 🔒 lock icon → enter password
  • → Download Source Report → check failed sources → manually collect missing stories if needed
  • → Read through all stories → click source links for any that seem incorrect → edit as needed
  • → Delete stories that are irrelevant or inappropriate
  • → Add any manually-collected stories not in the auto-generated list
  • → Reorder / re-categorize any misplaced stories using drag handles
  • → Publish — click ⬆ Publish button → verify in incognito tab
  • → Download Word → copy Page 1 titles → send to distribution group
✅ The entire review and publish process typically takes 15–30 minutes depending on the number of stories and how much editing is needed. The Word summary takes under a minute once the newsletter is finalized.
⚠️ If you notice the newsletter was not updated this week (e.g., stories are from a previous week), check with the Beijing team — the Python script may not have run, or the push to GitHub may have failed.
